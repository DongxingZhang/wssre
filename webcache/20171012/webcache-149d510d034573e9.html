Headline inflation slowed. The consumer price index (CPI) increased 2.1% y-o-y (HSBC: 2.3,BBG: 2.2) in September, slowing notably from 2.6% in August when fresh food prices kickedinflation higher. The rise in fresh food prices moderated from 18.3% y-o-y in August to 6.0% inSeptember despite the Chuseok holidays in early October, actually edging lower on a m-o-m basisafter seasonal adjustment by HSBC. Base effects from oil and domestic utility prices did not playlarge role in headline inflation in September, but are set to push it lower in the coming months.

Seasonally adjusted by HSBC, CPI increased 0.1% m-o-m sa in September, after rising 0.3% eachin the previous two months.

Core prices stable. Excluding agricultural and oil products, core CPI increased 1.6% (HSBC andBBG: 1.7) in September, slowing from 1.8% in both August and July. Service price inflation hasgradually moderated throughout the year to 1.8% y-o-y in September. Public service pricescontinue to drag down overall prices, but private services have also shown price gains moderate.

Meanwhile, the BoK's preferred measure of core inflation, excluding food and energy prices,increased 1.4% y-o-y for the second straight month in September.
